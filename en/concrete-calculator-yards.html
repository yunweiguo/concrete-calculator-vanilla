How to Calculate Concrete in Cubic Yards

Understanding Cubic Yards

A cubic yard is the standard unit for ordering ready-mix concrete. One cubic yard equals 27 cubic feet, which is approximately the amount of concrete that fits in a standard concrete truck.

Basic Formula

For rectangular shapes (slabs, foundations, walls):

Cubic Yards = (Length × Width × Thickness) ÷ 27

For Cylindrical Shapes

For columns and cylindrical footings:

Cubic Yards = (π × Radius² × Height) ÷ 27

Common Thicknesses

  • Driveways: 4-6 inches
  • Patios: 4 inches
  • Sidewalks: 4 inches
  • Foundations: 8-12 inches
  • Walls: 6-8 inches

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate concrete cubic yards?

Use our cubic yards calculator by entering the length, width, and thickness of your project. The calculator will automatically convert to cubic yards and provide material estimates.

How many cubic yards does a concrete truck hold?

Most concrete trucks can hold 10 cubic yards of concrete. For large projects, multiple truck loads may be required.

What's the difference between cubic feet and cubic yards?

1 cubic yard equals 27 cubic feet. Cubic yards are the standard unit for ordering ready-mix concrete from suppliers.

How much concrete for a 20x20 slab?

For a 20x20 slab with 4-inch thickness: 20 × 20 × (4/12) = 133.33 cubic feet = 4.94 cubic yards. Add 10% waste = 5.43 cubic yards.
